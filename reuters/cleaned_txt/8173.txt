FRENCH GDP SHOULD RISE 23 PCT IN 1988  MINISTRY
  French gross domestic product should grow
  by 23 pct in 1988 after two pct growth this year and 21 pct
  in 1986 the Finance Ministry said
      The latest forecast prepared by the National Accounts and
  Budget Commission assumed an exchange rate of 620 francs to
  the dollar this year and next and an average oil import price
  rising to 189 dlrs a barrel next year from 174 dlrs this year
  and 147 in 1986
      The Commission headed by Finance minister Edouard
  Balladur forecast a fall in consumer price inflation to two
  pct yearonyear at end1988 from 24 pct at end 1987 and 21
  pct last year
      In annual average terms inflation would fall to two pct in
  1988 from 25 pct this year and 27 pct last year it said
      Trade should show a one billion franc annual surplus this
  year and next after last years 05 billion surplus it added
      Employment should rise by 01 pct a year over the next two
  years while the state budget deficit should be cut to 22 pct
  of GDP in 1988 from 26 pct this year and 29 pct in 1986
      Other forecasts prepared by the Commission indicated a 18
  pct 1988 rise in household purchasing power up from 11 pct
  this year but less than last years 29 pct and a 16 pct rise
  in household consumption compared with this years 15 pct and
  last years 29 pct
      Business investment is forecast to rise four pct a year
  this year and next after 37 pct last year with private sector
  productive investment rising 49 pct in 1988 after a six pct
  rise this year and 55 pct in 1986
      The Ministry said updated forecasts would be prepared
  before the autumn to serve as the basis for the 1988 budget
  which the government is now preparing for presentation in
  September
