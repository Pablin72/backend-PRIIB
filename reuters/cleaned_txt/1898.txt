TAIWAN ISSUES MORE CDS TO CURB MONEY SUPPLY GROWTH
  The central bank has issued 708 billion
  dlrs worth of certificates of deposit CDs bringing the value
  of CD issues so far this year to 9329 billion a bank
  spokesman told Reuters
      The new CDs with maturities of six months one year and
  two years carry interest rates ranging from 39 to 515 pct
      The issues are designed to help curb the growth of the M1B
  money supply which has expanded along with Taiwans foreign
  exchange reserves the spokesman said The reserves reached a
  record high of more than 51 billion US Dlrs Wednesday
