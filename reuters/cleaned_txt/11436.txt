TRADERS EXPECT SHARP DROP IN US CORN ACREAGE
  Grain trade analysts expect a sharp
  drop in corn acreage with most expecting a more modest decline
  in soybeans in the US Agriculture Department USDA
  plantings intentions report due out at 1500 est 2100 gmt
      The average trade guess for 1987 planted corn acreage was
  6606 mln acres ranging from 607 to 690 mln all well below
  the 7667 mln planted last year due to improved incentives by
  the USDA for farmers to retire acres Corn acres totalled only
  6022 mln in 1983 when the PIK program was introduced
      The average soybean planted acreage quesstimate was 5949
  mln ranging from 580 to 630 mln and compared with the 6148
  mln planted in 1986
