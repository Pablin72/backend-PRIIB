SWISS SIGHT DEPOSITS FALL 288 BILLION FRANCS
  Sight deposits of commercial banks at
  the Swiss National Bank fell 288 billion Swiss francs in the
  first 10 days of March to 765 billion the National Bank said
      Foreign exchange reserves rose 330 billion francs to 3394
  billion
      Sight deposits are a major indicator of money market
  liquidity in Switzerland
      The National Bank said banks paid back 55 billion francs
  of central bank credit taken out at the end of February for the
  endmonth liquidity requirement
      This drain was offset in part by new currency swaps which
  had the effect of increasing the National Banks foreign
  exchange holdings
      Bank notes in circulation fell 3091 mln francs to 2449
  billion and other deposits on call  basically government
  funds  rose 106 billion to 210 billion
