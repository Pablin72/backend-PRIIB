INDIA AUSTRALIA AGREE TO IMPROVE TWOWAY TRADE
  Indian and Australian businessmen
  signed a memorandum of understanding to boost twoway trade and
  joint industrial ventures in both countries spokesmen of the
  IndoAustralia Business Council told reporters
      The Indian Council said it hoped Indias exports to
  Australia would improve in the next few months and reduce the
  trade balance which is heavily in Australias favour
      In 1986 Australia sold 428 mln Australian dlrs worth of
  goods including steel and coking coal to India and bought
  1705 mln worth of items including cotton textiles from India
