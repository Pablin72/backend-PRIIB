UK MONEY RATES HARDEN ON LAWSON CURRENCY TARGETS
  Money market rates rates showed a harder
  edge after news UK Chancellor of the Exchequer Nigel Lawson
  has set a target for the pound against the mark and dollar to
  be maintained by a mixture of interest rates and intervention
  in the foreign exchange markets
      Dealers said the market was surprised by the Chancellors
  disclosure of sterling target rates around 290 marks and 160
  dlrs and nervous over the implications for domestic interest
  rates further reducing the propects for a near term cut in
  UK Bank base rates The key three months interbank sterling
  rate gained 116 point at 10 978 pct
      The pound opened three basis points lower at 713 in trade
  weighted terms at around 159 dlrs and 291 marks
